The Mandir

Modern Architecture with Spiritual Roots

The Temple of Spirituality — a 300,000 sq. ft. sanctuary carrying 3,137 Swarved verses, crowned by a 125-petal lotus dome.

Architecture

An amalgamation of ancient philosophy and modern craft

Rising in seven storeys above the village of Umraha on the outskirts of Varanasi, the Mahamandir is clad in pink sandstone and adorned within by gleaming Makrana marble. Every pillar, every archway carries the mark of artisans who have offered their craft as worship.

Its sandstone architectural elements reflect classical Indian heritage, while its engineering supports one continuous meditation hall for over 20,000 sadhaks seated together in silence.

Inside the Mahamandir

Scripture carved in marble

3,137 Swarved verses line the walls — the only temple in the world where the scripture is the sanctum.

01

The Marble Verses

Every one of the 3,137 Swarved verses is engraved into Makrana marble — a meditation in itself to read them all.

02

20,000 Seats

A single continuous meditation hall that holds over twenty thousand sadhaks in a silence you can almost hear.

03

Seven Storeys

A seven-level superstructure spanning 300,000 square feet — each level dedicated to a different dimension of practice.

04

125-Petal Lotus Dome

The crowning dome is shaped as a hundred-and-twenty-five-petal lotus — a signature of the Mahamandir’s skyline.

05

Medicinal Gardens

The complex is ringed by extensive Ayurvedic herb gardens and a research centre for the study of conscious entities.

06

Free Entry, Always

No fee is ever charged — for meditation, darshan, worship or satsang. The Mahamandir is a non-profit initiative of the Sansthan.
